### Saturday

**Today's Progress**: 

Sorry for no updates this week, I kinda forgot I had to write these entries. So gear up for a long update.

 I don't know if I've talked about this before but I've had a serious addiction to YouTube for the past 4-5 years, I'm not someone who uses social media but this was basically my addiction equivalent to it, I've spent countless hours on that website, sitting on my ass (excuse the language) doing nothing productive, just watching every next YouTube video that showed up in my feed/suggestions, and now that I'm in the third year of my college I seriously was trying to find a way to help me be productive and get rid of this addiction and I'm happy to inform you that I've found a wonderful Chrome Extension for it, [DF Tube](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/df-tube-distraction-free/mjdepdfccjgcndkmemponafgioodelna?hl=en) is an extension that basically turn YouTube into a blank page so you don't really have anything to click on unless you are looking for something specifically. If you want to know how that helps this is basically what happens when you're watching YouTube,

>Every thumbnail, every click, every video gives you a little dopamine hit. Your brain rewards your behavior by releasing dopamine in the nucleus accumbens. This feels good and motivating, so you do it again, and again. One more video, and tomorrow anew. Over time, synaptic connections between neurons strengthen, a habit forms, and genes in the nucleus accumbens become overexpressed (specifically, ΔFosB). You become addicted. 
[(source)](https://www.mindcoolness.com/mastery/youtube-addiction/)

it's a fantastic extension as don't have an urge to watch something because I have no recommendations or suggestions in the first place, so I'm really happy about it (will update later as to if this really works or not).
